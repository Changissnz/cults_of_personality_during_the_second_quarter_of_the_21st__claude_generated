# Cults of Personality During the Second Quarter of the 21st

A fictional characterization of growing power bestowed to personality cults, during the emergence of large-scale artificial intelligence. Generated by Claude Sonnet 4.6.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Crumbs' ascent to the presidency was associated with cults: loyalty groups, the wildness of religiosity, and the eras of questioning capital worth that then meant the cherishing of human worth, personality flaws and quirks included. Despite the power of the American dollar as a global medium of exchange, there had become marked resistance to the dollar starting in the second quarter of the 21st century. Indeed, it was the lack of socioeconomic guarantees and the fallibility of independent merit that encouraged the organizing of political thought, among other important aspects of civic conduct, towards cult-associated activity.

---

In the right-wing, such loyalty falls under the umbrella of cults. The pseudo-scientific drive of the left-wing, a drive that many of its proponents desired to be scientific, de-emphasized the religiosity found in loyalty in favor of the fact-based acceptance of truths. But truth of humanity oftentimes demands its own representatives that selectively state truths. So most of the left-wing would not characterize their loyalties as cult-based. The terminology used is important to differentiate between these two wings. The fomenting cultism proved to be a much more unifying force than the decadence that left-wing secularity had regressed to. The phrasing of the left-wing regressing to a decadence, encouraged by secularity and the fallacies associated with misguidance under that umbrella of mores, surely would not have been wholeheartedly welcomed by their ranks. It is similar to pointing out a widely obvious security flaw: exploiting that security flaw is prohibited, but a mere mention of it would have encouraged more people to try it, like another child knocking over a domino in a vast and expansive project filled with perils inside and out.

This cooling of attitudes, predominant in Brittania, was only rational when the artificial intelligence waves were factored in. However, the artificial intelligence waves' opening of doors into cultural ultra-rationalism fell short of harmonizing the entire population of diversity. Artificial intelligence large-language models, for example, had limits in both objective and subjective tasks, sometimes devolving into word play and circular suggestions that made their users become more full of lunacy. Small worlds where human beings were supposed to be in the spotlight needed cults of personality. It was just that some cults still operated by more hierarchical structure than others. The success of some cults over others was loosely correlated to how hierarchical the structure was. But in general, hierarchical structures demanded ends be met, and ends were met under their supervision, typically of the right-wing. The left-wing, at several critical junctures during this period, had issues with not having more influential heads. The machinery of the left-wing's strengths, such as technology, had shifted towards either state-owned or wealth-owned enterprises during that time period. These shifts, for the most part, did not interrupt the more independent-minded developers, but did amount to trouble, resulting in egalitarian objectives going unmet.

Human initiatives required human representatives. Cults of personality can be fostered to give human societies, at large, the mental and visual models on how to act and what to think. Note that the artificial intelligence trends of this era would have encouraged a lot of subjectivity in the realms of thought. From the lens of perception, intelligent machines could do the thinking. They could also do the acting. So what would human beings do, given so much time and effort being freed up for their own doing? There would have certainly still needed to be role models, but perhaps that phrasing is not completely correct due to the universal fallibility of individuals. But there would have still needed to be human beings that determined what was right and wrong, and the forms by which to live and act. In the categorical outlining of black and white, whereas the left-wing prized itself on secularity and rationality that ended up dictating the acceptable forms by which human beings can live, the right-wing came to prize itself on the forms by which human beings should act. This dichotomy, by most observable metrics, naturally favored the strengths of the right-wing, as actors of and for A.I., over the left-wing, theorists and ideological half-puritans full-hypocrites, especially due to the constraining of resources in many important categories during that time period. The solutions of the left-wing did not fully help their cause. They tried to fight cultism by promoting human representatives of anti-cultism, which in turn was basically a different side of the same coin.
